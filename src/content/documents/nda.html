Non-Disclosure Agreement

Between Reave and {client.name}{client.company_str}  ·  {date}

1. Parties

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is made as of {date} between Reave ("Disclosing Party") and {client.name}{client.company_str} ("Receiving Party"). Together referred to as the "Parties."

Receiving Party contact: {client.email}

2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by Disclosing Party to {client.first_name}, including but not limited to: business strategies, pricing, client lists, source code, design files, trade secrets, and any information marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the context of disclosure.

3. Obligations of the Receiving Party

{client.name} agrees to:

  • Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence.
  • Not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent.
  • Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating or engaging in a business relationship with Disclosing Party.
  • Limit internal access to Confidential Information on a need-to-know basis.

4. Exclusions

These obligations do not apply to information that:

  • Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by {client.name}.
  • Was already known to Receiving Party prior to disclosure.
  • Is independently developed by {client.name} without use of Confidential Information.
  • Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (with prompt notice to Disclosing Party).

5. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of three (3) years from {date}, unless earlier terminated in writing by both Parties.

6. Return of Information

Upon request or termination, {client.name} shall promptly return or certify destruction of all materials containing Confidential Information.

7. No License

Nothing in this Agreement grants {client.name} any rights, title, or license in or to any Confidential Information, intellectual property, or technology of Disclosing Party.

8. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the jurisdiction in which Disclosing Party operates, effective {year}. Breach of this Agreement may result in irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate, entitling Disclosing Party to seek injunctive relief.
